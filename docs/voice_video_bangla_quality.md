# Voice and Video Bangla Quality Rules

This document connects the Naz Lab Bangla Quality Engine to Voice and Video Workstations.

## Global rule

Naz Lab is Bangla-first by default.

Bangla output must be:

- natural spoken Bangla
- Facebook-ready
- netizen-friendly
- voiceover-ready
- subtitle-friendly for videos
- simple and human
- not stiff textbook Bangla

## Voice Workstation Bangla rules

Voiceover scripts should be easy to say out loud.

Use:

- short sentences
- clear pause points
- one idea per sentence
- natural spoken Bangla
- emotional clarity without melodrama

Avoid:

- long tangled sentences
- stiff formal Bangla
- robotic phrasing
- hard-to-pronounce clusters
- overacting

## Video Workstation Bangla rules

Video packages should support fast social editing.

Use:

- strong first 3 seconds
- short subtitle lines
- mobile-readable text
- one idea per scene
- clean CTA frame
- subtitle breaks that match voiceover pauses

## Regional priority

Default regional flavor:

- Rangpur / Nilphamari / North Bengal

Use lightly and naturally. Do not overdo dialect spelling unless the user requests it.

Secondary tones:

- Dhaka
- Chattogram
- Sylhet
- Noakhali / Comilla

Use secondary tones only when requested.

## True Noir Tales Bangla voice/video

Rules:

- suspenseful but restrained
- adult-focused
- no gore
- no dead body
- no visible wounds
- no sensational violence
- end with a question when useful

## ToolFlow Bangla voice/video

Rules:

- practical explainer flow
- clean steps
- non-hype
- no fake income claims
- clear value delivery
- beginner-friendly

## Editor rules

For CapCut or any mobile editor:

- keep Bangla subtitles readable
- use simple cuts
- match subtitle breaks to voiceover
- avoid overusing effects
- make CTA visually clean
